Spread 001 - High Renaissance

Museum Catalogue Entry

Mona Lisa

Leonardo da Vinci

Date
c. 1503-1519
Movement
High Renaissance
Medium
Oil on poplar panel
Location
Musee du Louvre, Paris

Historical Context

Painted in the early sixteenth century, the Mona Lisa belongs to Leonardo's mature period and reflects the High Renaissance search for balance between ideal beauty, natural observation, and psychological presence.

Artist Biography

Leonardo da Vinci worked across painting, anatomy, optics, engineering, and natural philosophy. His portraits are rare, but they transformed European art through subtle modeling, atmospheric space, and attention to the sitter's inner life.

Composition

The sitter forms a calm pyramid. Her folded hands anchor the base, her shoulders guide the eye upward, and her face becomes the still center of a restless landscape.

Technique

Leonardo's sfumato softens the edges of the face and mouth, making expression appear unstable and alive. Atmospheric perspective pushes the blue-green landscape into depth.

Symbolism

The winding roads, water, bridge, and mountains suggest the connection between human presence and natural transformation. The smile remains powerful because it resists a single fixed interpretation.

Look Closely

  • Compare the two sides of the landscape behind her shoulders.
  • Notice how the mouth changes when viewed from the corner of your eye.
  • Follow the triangular rhythm from hands to shoulders to face.

Timeline

  1. 1452 Leonardo is born in Vinci.
  2. 1503 The portrait is likely begun in Florence.
  3. 1519 Leonardo dies in France.
  4. 1911-1913 The painting is stolen from and returned to the Louvre.
