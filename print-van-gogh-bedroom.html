Spread 003 - Post-Impressionism

Museum Catalogue Entry

The Bedroom

Vincent van Gogh

Date
1888
Movement
Post-Impressionism
Medium
Oil on canvas
Theme
Interior, rest, artistic identity

Historical Context

Van Gogh painted The Bedroom in Arles after moving into the Yellow House. The room represented rest, stability, and the hope of creating an artist's home in the south of France.

Artist Biography

In Arles, Van Gogh searched for a direct art of strong color and emotional sincerity. Everyday subjects became charged with meaning, and his own bedroom became a portrait of inner life.

Color Palette

Flat areas of blue, lavender, yellow, orange, green, and red make the room vivid. Van Gogh wanted the colors to suggest rest, but their contrasts keep the image visually active.

Perspective

The furniture tilts forward and the walls seem to lean. This simplified perspective is expressive rather than academic, making the room feel personal and emotionally unstable.

Symbolism

The bed, chairs, portraits, towels, and window suggest rest, friendship, cleanliness, privacy, and the promise of a settled artistic life.

Look Closely

  • Follow the floorboards into the depth of the room.
  • Compare the two empty chairs and what they suggest.
  • Notice how the bed dominates the right side of the composition.

Timeline

  1. 1853 Van Gogh is born in Zundert, Netherlands.
  2. 1888 He moves to Arles and rents rooms in the Yellow House.
  3. 1888 He paints The Bedroom as an image of rest and private space.
  4. 1889 He paints later versions of the bedroom composition.
